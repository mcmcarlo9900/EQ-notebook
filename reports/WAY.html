01 What the Company Does

Waystar runs the plumbing of US healthcare billing. Hospitals, physician groups, and ambulatory providers use its cloud platform to submit claims to insurers, verify patient eligibility, appeal denials, collect patient payments, and reconcile remittances — the entire revenue cycle from patient scheduling to cash in account. The Iodine acquisition (Oct 2025, ~$1B) added the mid-cycle layer: clinical documentation integrity (CDI), which ensures physician notes accurately capture patient complexity before claims are submitted. This expanded Waystar from a post-service billing platform into a pre-to-post platform spanning financial clearance → clinical documentation → claim submission → denial recovery → patient payment.

The US healthcare system processes roughly $4 trillion in annual claims. The administrative overhead — billing, coding, authorization, collection — costs providers an estimated $350B/year in labor and error. Waystar's business is replacing that cost with software.

Why Is Subscription Growing Faster Than Volume?

1. Iodine is subscription-only. The $1B acquisition (Oct 2025) added 1,000+ hospitals on pure subscription contracts — zero volume revenue. This alone drove the gap from ~0ppt to 27ppt in one quarter.

2. New AI products are priced as subscriptions. AltitudeAI, CDI products, and prior auth tools sold on per-seat or per-module subscription — not per-transaction. As AI bookings hit 40% of new deals, the new unit mix skews subscription.

3. Patient payment volume is normalizing. Post-COVID elevated utilization drove volume above trend. That tailwind is fading — patient pay is now ~25% of revenue (was ~30%), dragging volume growth down.

4. Health systems prefer subscription. CFOs prefer predictable budgets. Platform deals convert point-solution volume users onto all-inclusive subscription contracts, showing up as NRR expansion rather than new logos.

The product suite spans five functional stages: Financial Clearance (eligibility, prior auth, patient estimation), Clinical Integrity (CDI, coding, DRG validation via Iodine), Claim Management (submission, monitoring, Medicare), Payment Management (ERA posting, patient payments), and Denial Recovery (AI-assisted appeals). 02 Investment Thesis — First Principles
Pillar 1 — The Moat Is Financial Impact + Regulation + Accuracy. AI Amplifies All Three.
This is not a data moat. It is a compliance-accuracy-consequence moat. Data is the mechanism, not the reason.

Financial impact: Every dollar of a hospital's revenue flows through RCM. A 1% collection rate improvement on a $1B system is $10M. Switching vendors means 12-18 months of cash flow risk during re-implementation. That is why gross retention is 97% even when competitors underprice.

Regulation: ICD-10/11, CMS rules, HIPAA, 1,000+ payer contracts, state-level prior auth mandates — all change constantly. Every change requires a platform update. A new entrant does not face a one-time build; it faces a permanent compliance treadmill Waystar has been running for 20+ years. The compliance layer is inherited by being the incumbent, not replicable from scratch.

Accuracy + data: Miscoded claim → denial → appeal → revenue loss. Accuracy requires payer-specific training data. That data is produced only by processing billions of real claims. A better foundation model cannot replicate it without the scale. Scale requires being Waystar.

The chain: Regulation → complexity → accuracy → data → scale → incumbency → financial impact. AI tightens every link — higher-accuracy AI requires more proprietary data, which requires more throughput, which deepens the moat. AltitudeAI drives 40% of new bookings while making existing clients harder to displace simultaneously.

Pillar 2 — Change Healthcare Permanently Changed How Hospitals Buy RCM
Hospitals learned they cannot have a single RCM vendor. That procurement shift expands the TAM.

Feb 2024: UnitedHealth's Change Healthcare — ~40% of US claims — went offline for weeks from ransomware. Hospital cash flows froze. Waystar onboarded 30,000+ providers in 48 hours, proving resilience and permanently changing CFO procurement logic: 2-3 RCM vendors minimum, not 1.

TAM expansion: The addressable market is now (organizations × contract value × 1.5-2x) because each organization wants redundancy. This is not share capture from Change — it is genuine TAM growth. Iodine adds a second layer by pulling CDI/clinical intelligence into the TAM that was not there before.

The 2024 bookings wave seeded a multi-year implementation backlog still flowing into revenue. NRR spiked to 115% in Q2 2025 as those clients reached full LTM recognition. Implementation queue is the largest in company history.

Pillar 3 — Valuation Is Structurally Dislocated by One Temporary Factor
Rule of 50 = 65. EV/EBITDA = 10x. The gap has one explanation: leverage.

Top-quintile software by Rule of 50 (22% growth + 43% margin = 65) typically trades at 20-30x EBITDA. WAY trades at 10x. The sole explanation is 2.7x net leverage — institutional mandates exclude or underweight leveraged software automatically. The eligible buyer pool is mechanically compressed, not fundamentally impaired.

The re-rating requires no business improvement — only time and EBITDA compounding. At 1x/year delevering, WAY is sub-2x by 2027. At 18x EBITDA (still below quality comps) on $535M FY2026E EBITDA: $40+/share. Current price ~$21 is the wait.

Moody's and S&P both upgraded Q1 2026. The rating agencies have already reached this conclusion.

What Would Break This Thesis
Three conditions under which this is wrong.

(1) Epic vertical integration. Epic controls the EHR layer. Aggressive native RCM bundling compresses Waystar's enterprise TAM. Slow erosion, not displacement — but real.

(2) Medicaid cuts impair provider budgets. Federal cuts reduce hospital revenue. Budget freezes do not always follow ROI logic in hospital procurement cycles.

(3) Iodine integration fails. No cross-sell into the existing base, or key talent loss, means $1B of leverage incurred for zero strategic return. Watch Iodine revenue run-rate and cross-sell bookings as the leading indicator each quarter.

04 Current Debate
Bear
Leverage limits capital flexibility and is a permanent overhang
At 2.7x net leverage with $1.5B gross debt, Waystar has limited room to do M&A, buy back stock, or absorb a revenue miss. The Iodine acquisition ($1B) was executed at IPO-era multiples for an asset that added integration risk. Interest expense is a real P&L drag. Bears argue the stock deserves a discount to peers like Veeva or Evolent for this reason alone.
Bull
Leverage is self-healing — 1 turn/year is the base case, not a hope
At $535M FY2026E EBITDA and 67% unlevered FCF conversion, Waystar generates ~$360M in unlevered FCF annually. Debt is term loan structure. At current pace (3.4x → 2.7x in 18 months), the company hits sub-2x by 2027/2028. Two credit rating upgrades in Q1 2026 confirm the trajectory. The leverage is temporary; the business quality is permanent. Moody's and S&P are effectively the bull case made official.
Bear
Volume-based revenue is cyclical and exposed to payer policy risk
45% of revenue is volume-based — tied to claims transaction volume and patient payment throughput. A recession that reduces elective procedure volume, or payer policy changes (e.g. prior auth reform that reduces transaction count) could cause volume revenue to stall or decline. Q1 2026 volume growth was only +7% vs. subscription's +38%, supporting this concern.
Bull
Subscription mix shift is structural and accelerating
The Q1 2026 mix (55% subscription vs. 45% volume) was 50/50 a year ago. Iodine accelerates this — clinical intelligence products are priced as subscriptions. Management's platform consolidation strategy pulls clients toward all-inclusive subscription contracts. Each percentage point gained in subscription mix lifts NRR durability and reduces cyclicality. This is a multi-year story, not a one-quarter event.
Bear
AI disrupts the denial management wedge (the core moat)
If AI commoditizes claim submission and denial appeals, point solutions could undercut Waystar on price. Open-source LLMs trained on claims data could eventually erode the data moat. The "autonomous revenue cycle" narrative benefits incumbents today — but could benefit challengers in a world where foundation models flatten the playing field.
Bull
AI amplifies Waystar's data advantage, not destroys it
AltitudeAI (Waystar's denial appeal AI) reduces a 38-hour process to 2 hours — delivering 40% of new bookings already. The key insight: the data needed to train effective healthcare AI (payer-specific denial patterns, coding accuracy benchmarks, authorization decision trees) is exactly the data Waystar has accumulated across thousands of provider relationships over decades. A point solution cannot replicate that training corpus. AI makes Waystar stickier, not more replaceable.
Bear
Healthcare policy uncertainty (Medicaid, Medicare cuts) hurts client budgets
Federal budget pressures and Medicaid restructuring (potential cuts) reduce hospital system revenue. If provider clients face revenue pressure, software budgets could freeze. Waystar's ROI is high, but budget freezes do not always follow ROI logic in hospital administration cycles.
05 Valuation

WAY trades at a deep discount to healthcare IT peers despite superior growth and margin. The discount is almost entirely explained by leverage optics — which are self-correcting — and post-IPO lock-up selling (stock is down ~53% from its all-time closing high of $45.35 in Feb 2025).

WAY vs. IGV — Indexed to 100 at IPO (Jun 2024)
WAY ran to 2x the IPO price by Feb 2025 as Change Healthcare drove bookings acceleration — massively outperforming software peers. Then Iodine was announced (Jul 2025), leverage reset to 3.4x, and WAY gave back all gains. YTD 2026: WAY is down ~33% vs. IGV down ~24%. The fundamental story has improved materially since IPO; the stock has not moved.

Why Is C26E Revenue Growth Only 17% If Iodine Is That Transformative?

Iodine closed Oct 2025 — so FY2025 already includes ~2 months of Iodine revenue. The C26E base of $1,099M is not a clean pre-Iodine number. C26 management guidance of 17% = ~10% organic (management's stated normalized rate) + ~7ppt from annualizing Iodine's remaining 10 months. The lift is real but already in the run-rate. The incremental Iodine story in C26/C27 is cross-sell into 1,000+ Iodine hospitals — that shows up as new bookings and NRR expansion, not headline growth rate acceleration.
